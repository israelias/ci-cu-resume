About

Who i am

I am a transnational subject (an expat baby) — raised in seven cities across the Pacific by the age of seventeen. As a result, I am always grounded at the intersections between the local and the global. As a creative, I appreciate the opportunities and constraints of trade expertises and material techniques unique to the intertwined locales of Site. As a professional, I capitalize on the collective knowledge of global networks while remaining embedded to a conscientious approach to design, representation, entrepreneurialism and leadership.

What i do

I am a design architect (buildings) with 7 years of professional experience. I worked with a large firms in Los Angeles, New York, Basel and Singapore after receiving my Master of Architecture from Columbia University. I am also a design technologist (computers) and presently in pursuit of a Diploma in Software Development at Code Institute.

Why i do it

I am eager to be on the backend of the design delivery. I believe the most essential skill — for the building industry or otherwise — is to be able to think and produce algorithmically. I fundamentally enjoy programming and find the practice of writing code extremely rewarding. I want to be fully capable of contributing to open source. I want to develop programs that are useful, educational and aesthetically pleasing in its finished deployment as well as its procedural delivery. I want to continue to be adaptable to new languages in order to grow, operate and contribute across platforms effectively and consistently.

Misc.

My research interests are on responsive design, the computational design of home automation, space optimization, virtual reality visualization and performance control. In my spare time, I enjoy reviewing films, reading memoirs, designing books and playing the piano.
